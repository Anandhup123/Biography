Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

The Missile Man of India

Life & Legacy

Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam (1931-2015) was a visionary leader, scientist, and the 11th President of India. Renowned as the "Missile Man of India," his contributions propelled India's aerospace program to new heights.

Born in a humble background, Dr. Kalam's dedication and hard work led him to a distinguished career in aerospace engineering. He played a pivotal role in developing India's indigenous missile technology, including the Agni and Prithvi series.

Key Achievements

  • Led the development of India's ballistic missile and launch vehicle programs.
  • Played a crucial role in Pokhran-II nuclear tests, solidifying India's nuclear deterrence.
  • Championed scientific education and youth empowerment, inspiring generations.
  • Served as the 11th President of India (2002-2007), beloved for his humility and vision.
